The Time Domain Explorer

A SMEX mission concept that would launch in the mid-2020's, Nimble addresses key questions in astrophysics through rapid response, high precision, multiwavelength observations.

Understand the nature of binary neutron star mergers

Nimble detects short GRB prompt emission and follows-up kilonovae to understand binary neutron star mergers.

Characterize exoplanets and their atmospheres

When not chasing transients, Nimble observes exoplanets transiting bright stars to measure stellar activity, refine planet parameters, and characterize atmospheres.

The Nimble Mission
Addresses Decadal Survey and NASA Science priorities.
Builds on the heritage of Swift and other NASA missions.
Complements ground based transient surveys and JWST.
Is a flexible platform capable of wide ranging science.

Instruments

High-energy All-sky Monitor (HAM)
  • HAM detects and localizes the short GRB.
  • CsI scintillators, Si photomultipliers.
  • 10 - 1000 keV energy sensitivity.
  • 5 deg radius source localization.
Small UV-Optical-IR telescope (SUVOIR)
  • SUVOIR identifies kilonova using its wide-FOV mode and obtains simultaneous multiwavelength light curves/spectra in its narrow-FOV mode.
  • SUVOIR narrow-FOV mode observes transits from the NUV to NIR to characterize host stars, planets, and atmospheres
